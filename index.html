🩹 First Aid Information

Common Issues

Blisters: Clean, apply blister plaster, keep dry

Sprains: Rest, ice, compression, elevation (RICE)

Headache: Hydrate, rest in cool area, paracetamol if needed

Dehydration Signs

• Thirst and dry mouth

• Dizziness or lightheadedness

• Dark urine

Response: Move to shade, drink water slowly

Alcohol-Related Issues

• Monitor their breathing and responsiveness. If they become unconscious, difficult to wake, or have slowed breathing, call 112 immediately.

• Water is fine. Food is okay if fully conscious, but don’t encourage giving food to semi-conscious intoxicated people due to choking risk.

• Monitor for excessive intoxication

• Place unconscious but breathing people in the recovery position.

• Call 112 if unresponsive

Allergic Reactions

• Ask about known allergies

• Keep antihistamines available

• Call 112 for severe reactions

• EpiPens for severe cases

Heat Exhaustion

• Move to cool location

• Remove excess clothing

• Apply cool, wet cloths

• Drink water with electrolytes

Minor Injuries

• Stop bleeding with pressure

• Clean wound with water

• Apply antibiotic ointment

• Cover with sterile bandage

🟢 Cannabis/Weed - Greening Out

Greening out is usually not life-threatening and often improves with reassurance, hydration, and rest. However, call 112 if the person is unconscious, having breathing difficulties, chest pain, seizures, severe confusion, or if you are seriously concerned. ⚠️

This is not usually a medical emergency. Contact Jack or a sober person for guidance.

Common Symptoms (normal & will pass):

• Dizziness, nausea, or vomiting

• Anxiety or panic feeling

• Sweating or chills

• A fast heartbeat is common after cannabis use and often settles with reassurance and rest.

• Feeling faint or lightheaded

These symptoms are usually temporary and improve with reassurance and rest.

What to do immediately:

1. Contact Jack (designated first aider) or a sober committee member

2. Move to a quiet, safe location away from crowds

3. Sit or lie down in a comfortable position

4. Reassure the person - remind them it will pass

5. Have them breathe slowly and deeply

6. Provide water or a sugary drink (juice/soft drink)

7. Keep them in a cool, dark space if possible

Recovery: Symptoms typically pass within 1-3 hours with rest and calm support. Stay with the person the entire time, or until help arrives.

❌ ONLY call 112 if:

• Person loses consciousness and cannot be woken

• Chest pain (not panic-related)

• Difficulty breathing that doesn't improve with calm breathing

• Suspected seizure or convulsions

GREENING OUT ALONE IS NOT A 112 EMERGENCY
